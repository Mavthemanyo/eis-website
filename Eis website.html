01 — About

Markets, taught by students,
for students.

EIS started as a handful of preps trading paper portfolios in the Elm Street common room. Today it's Exeter's home for market literacy — a place to learn how capital actually moves, and to test your own thinking against people who'll happily tell you why you're wrong.

We run weekly meetings that mix market news, company deep-dives, and open debate — no finance background required. Members who want to go further can join our stock pitch teams, sit on the portfolio committee, or represent Exeter at regional case competitions.

The society is entirely member-managed. Officers are elected each spring, meeting topics are member-proposed, and the student-run portfolio is voted on by the group — every position sized and defended in front of your peers before a dollar moves.

02 — Programs

Four ways to get involved.

Come for one meeting or dive into all of it — every track is open to every member, every term.

01

Stock Pitch Teams

Small teams research a company, build a thesis, and pitch it live to the club — winning pitches get added to the portfolio watchlist.

02

Speaker Series

Alumni in finance and investing join us throughout the year for candid conversations about markets, careers, and mistakes worth learning from.

03

Trading Simulation

A running paper-trading competition open to all members, with a leaderboard and a small prize for the strongest term.

04

Case Competitions

We field teams for regional investment and case competitions each year, coached by our senior analysts.

05

Portfolio Committee

A rotating group of members who track, debate, and vote on every position in the society's real student-managed fund.

06

Market Digest

A member-written weekly digest covering the news, moves, and numbers worth knowing before Thursday's meeting.
